What is Income Inequality?

Income inequality refers to how evenly or unevenly income is distributed in a society. When inequality is high, a small group of people earn most of the income.

In an equal society, everyone has the same income. A uniformly rich country is just as equal as one in which everyone is poor.

Gathering Income Data

The Census Bureau surveys, analyzes and reports on income inequality in the United States every year.

Income inequality can be measured by:

  • Comparing the share of wealth held by households with different incomes
  • Calculating single-figure statistical indexes (like the Gini coefficient)
